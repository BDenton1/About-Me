Where it Started:

The first time I rode a bike for a non-transportational purpose in my neighborhood at the age
of four. My dad took me to a small track next to me school. Looking back, I was incredibly lucky
to have something so cool in my neighborhood. This track would become my home over the next eight
years. Sometime after I turned eight, I started racing BMX competitively. I would travel all over
the country with a great team of kids, and it was a lot of fun. However, I was more interested in
catching air and doing tricks. My first freestyle bike came when I was about ten years old, but I
continued racing until I was fourteen. Once I got on that freestyle bike I knew I was never getting
off. It has been more than ten years and I still cannot get enough of it.

Influence:

The first rider I ever looked up to is Aaron Ross. His part in the "Chill Bro" DVD was legendary and
inspired me the instant I saw it. Today, my favorite riders are Simone Barraco, Mark Burnett, and
Devon Smillie.

Goals:

I have always dreamed of riding professionally, but now that I am entering my twenties my priorities
are starting to shift. I still intend to push myself, but if I can simply capture my progress on
film, then I will have all the accolades I want.
